Arsenic Fibrosis Progression AI Model

Arsenic-Induced Cardiac Stress

Prolonged environmental arsenic exposure can lead to intracellular stress, causing myocardial damage that triggers cardiac fibroblast activation. This results in progressive extracellular matrix accumulation (scarring/fibrosis) and heart failure.

Diagnostic Biomarkers Parsed

Our deep neural network analyzes diagnostic reports for three critical biochemical indices:

Troponin I (cTnI) Cardiac muscle injury marker. Healthy range: < 0.04 ng/mL. Levels rise as myofibrils suffer damage.
BNP & NT-proBNP Myocardial stretch markers. In response to volume and pressure strain, ventricles release BNP. Healthy ranges: BNP < 100 pg/mL, NT-proBNP < 125 pg/mL.

Progression Risk Calculation

The progression risk algorithm blends biomarker elevation curves with clinical histories (e.g. pre-existing hypertension or direct arsenic exposure history) using a neural regression classifier. Results categorize users into Healthy, Low Risk, Risk, or High Risk, helping cardiologists formulate early intervention plans.
